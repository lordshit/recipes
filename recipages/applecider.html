Apple Cider Vinegar!

Home

Description

Apple cider vinegar, or cider vinegar, is a vinegar made from cider, and used in salad dressings, marinades, vinaigrettes, food preservatives, and chutneys.Making apple cider vinegar is surprisingly easy and very similar to brewing kombucha or cider!

Ingredients

  • Apples
  • Water
  • Sugar or Honey

Steps

  1. Chop 4-5 rinsed apples into roughly 1–2 in (2.5–5.1 cm) pieces.
  2. Add the apples to a sterilized 64 oz (~2 L) glass jar, with cool water.
  3. Cover the jar with a cheesecloth.
  4. Place the jar in a warm, dark place for 3-4 weeks and stir it once a day.
  5. Strain the apples out of the mixture when it smells like vinegar.
  6. Return the liquid to the fermenting jar and let it sit for another 2 weeks.
  7. Strain and store the vinegar in a sterilized jar. After 2 weeks, your apple cider vinegar’s all done!

Description

Glycerin, also known as glycerol, is a colorless, odorless, sweet-tasting liquid that's commonly used in cosmetics, medicines, and food.Glycerin can be used as a moisturiser, solvent, and as a sweetener in food and beverages. This compound can also help preserve certain foods.

  • 1 lb (450 g) animal fat
  • 1⁄4 cup (59 ml) water
  • 1 lb (450 g) rendered fat
  • 2 ounces (57 g) lye
  • 5 fluid ounces (150 ml) water
  • 1 cup (300 g) salt

Steps

  1. Purchase or gather around 1 lb (450 g) of animal fat.
  2. Cut the fat into 1 in (2.5 cm) cubes.
  3. Add the fat and water to a large stock pot.
  4. Cover the pot and cook over low heat for 30 minutes.
  5. Increase the heat to medium and stir the fat every few minutes.
  6. Strain the rendered fat through a fine sieve and cheesecloth.
  7. Weigh out 1 lb (450 g) of rendered fat into a large pot.
  8. Stir 2 ounces (57 g) of lye into 5 fluid ounces (150 ml) of water.
  9. Bring the fat to 113 °F (45 °C) and place it on a heat proof surface.
  10. Pour the lye solution into the melted fat slowly, stirring constantly.
  11. Mix the fat and lye together until the soap begins ‘tracing’.
  12. Add 1 cup (300 g) of salt to the soap mixture.
  13. Leave the mixture to cool and curdle.
  14. Skim away the soap from the top of the mixture.
  15. Strain the remaining glycerin into a glass bottle to store.
